Tom's Thoughts

  • There has to be a balance between your mental satisfaction and the financial needs of your company. The only way to do something in depth is to work hard. I try to contrast; life today is full of contrast... We have to change. I love the 2000s because everyone started to love haute couture. Over the years I have learned that what is important in a suit is the person who is wearing it.
  • I wish I had invented blue jeans. They have expression, modesty, sex appeal, simplicity - all I hope for in my clothes. I get ideas about what's essential when packing my suitcase. Pawnee taught me that not everything is glamorous. Sometimes the simplest things are the most profound.
  • Luxury must be comfortable, otherwise it is not luxury. Everything I do is a matter of heart, body and soul. I have a fantastic relationship with money. I use it to buy my freedom. We must never confuse elegance with snobbery. I like the irony in my work.
